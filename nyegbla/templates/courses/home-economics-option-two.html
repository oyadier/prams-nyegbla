Home Economics (Clothing) at Prampram Senior High (PSH)


Are you excited about fashion, design, and practical life skills? Do you want to explore the fascinating world of clothing, textiles, and management in a hands-on, interactive environment? If so, the Home Economics (Clothing) course at Prampram Senior High (PSH) is the ideal choice for you!

Prampram Senior High offers a comprehensive Home Economics program based on the new Standard-Based Curriculum designed by the National Council for Curriculum and Assessment (NaCCa). This ensures that the course aligns with modern industry standards and prepares students for real-world success. The Home Economics (Clothing) course is structured to focus 70% on practical learning, providing you with valuable hands-on experience that complements the theoretical knowledge gained in the classroom. With over 10 years of experience in teaching this course, the dedicated staff at PSH will guide you through every step of your learning journey.

The program is designed to give students the opportunity to explore two main areas: Home Economics (Clothing) and Home Economics (Food). While this overview focuses on the Clothing option, both tracks offer vital skills for personal and professional growth.

Skills You Will Acquire:


  • Expertise in sewing, garment making, and textile design.
  • Ability to design and create clothing and accessories with a deep understanding of fabrics and patterns.
  • Knowledge of sustainable practices in textile production.
  • Insight into business management, equipping you to run or manage a clothing business or textile-related enterprise.
  • Practical time management, communication, and problem-solving skills.
  • Preparation for entrepreneurship or leadership roles within the fashion or textile industries.

Subjects Covered


  1. Art and Design: Explore your creative potential while learning essential design principles, helping you develop a unique style and understanding of fashion trends.
  2. Clothing and Textiles: Gain in-depth knowledge of fabric types, garment construction, and fashion design. Learn the skills to create your own clothing and understand the textile industry.
  3. Management in Living: Discover the principles of home management, including budgeting, time management, and resource planning—skills that will help you excel in both personal and professional aspects of life.
  4. Business Management: Learn the fundamentals of business, including entrepreneurship, marketing, and operational strategies, especially as they relate to the fashion and textile industries.

Career Opportunities:


Completing the Home Economics (Clothing) course at PSH will open doors to a wide range of exciting career paths, including:

  1. Fashion designer or clothing stylist.
  2. Textile technician or fabric consultant.
  3. Costume designer for theater or film.
  4. Entrepreneur in the fashion or textile industry.
  5. Fashion merchandising or retail management.
  6. Clothing production supervisor.
  7. Fashion marketing or sales professional.
  8. Home economics educator or consultant.

Conclusion

Choosing PSH for your Home Economics (Clothing) course means you’ll receive expert training in a well-rounded, practical environment.
